Disaster Management Final Project

How can social media be deployed in warning communities?



  1. How can any community be engaged in Early Warning Systems (EWS) to save lives and to reduce injuries?
  2. What could be the different forms of social media that could be used in disaster management focusing on volunteer groups and organizations?
  3. Detect and mitigate the spread of fake news surrounding a disaster on social media.
  4. Try to adapt existing mesh network messaging architectures to be deployed for communication during disasters.



Developed by Swaraj Renghe, Shreyas Shankar and Sriven Reddy



Reason to select project

All three of the team members have been working with LTRC lab for our honors project, hence we chose a project that can best exploit our skillset. We feel like we can use some NLP techniques like sentiment analysis, semantic word disambiguation and other techniques to better collaborate between volunteer groups, to better understand local situations and to provide immediate support to affected individuals.

Challenge One

Lack of Means of Reliable Communication

Immediately following a disaster, most means of communication like phone networks and internet service providers will be non-operational.

Challenge Two

Spread of Fake News

In the aftermath of a disaster, there’s rampant spread of fake and propaganda news spread on social media, that can have adverse effects on relief efforts, and people’s morale.

Challenge Three

Awareness about Disasters

Given social media content, like tweets or FB posts, this challenge is about recognizing which of them are talking about a disaster event that took place, using AI.

Objectives

Disaster Tweet Identification

Twitter has become an important communication channel in times of emergency. The iniquitousness of smartphones enables people to announce an emergency they’re observing in real-time. Because of this, disaster relief organizations and news agencies are interested in programmatically monitoring Twitter. Unfortunately, it’s usually a difficult task to correctly identify a valid disaster tweet.


Fake News Classifier

The unmoderated nature of sites such as Twitter can create a rumor mill among users. When false information is maliciously or mistakenly spread on social media sites it can result in millions of misinformed users. Over the last decade, there has been an overwhelming amount of cases where false news spreads during crisis events, such as natural disasters and terrorist attacks. We will try to build a method of a lookup on reputed new sources to verify or refute claims made, and classify them as fake or real.


Mesh Broadcast Network

In mesh networks, messages bypass the internet by using protocols that allow devices to talk to each other – eg. Bluetooth or an Ad Hoc wifi network running on the device. The idea of localised communication that needs no external infrastructure is very useful during disasters that might destroy existing cell towers and underground cables cutting off people from accessing new information about the disaster. A light weight app might be built to allow communication during disasters and to allow the government to direct citizens to safety and provide relief and information about rescue.
