Monitoring Disasters and Accidents through Twitter

Twitter, and other social media sites have become important communication channel in times of emergencies. As smartphones become more ubiquitous, it becomes imperative to monitor these outlets.


Law enforcement agencies, disaster relief organizations and news agencies are interested in programmatically monitoring Twitter. These become quick methods of identifying smaller scale disasters and risks.


Unfortunately, it’s usually a difficult task to correctly identify a valid disaster tweet.


We aim to use state-of-the-art NLP models to help disaster authorities identify occurrences of disasters immediately, by scouring social media for posts by citizens caught in a disaster. While local governments and relief organizations can measure a community’s ability to respond to a disaster or measure its impacts after, they cannot monitor the effects in real time. Several studies have shown that supplementing social media data can be a real advantage to first responders. Depending on the performance of the model, it may even be possible to deploy this aspect of the project in helping disaster management authorities in the country.
